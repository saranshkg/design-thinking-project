Online Class Techniques

Add new Technique
Preferred Techniques
S No. Technique Description
1. Tap that Camera To bridge the gap and encourage students to switch on their cameras, the following techniques can be applied: Fastest Person Challenge, Zoom Background & Filter, Physical Movements, Ice Breakers.
2. All-inclusive strategies This strategy focuses on maximising engagement of all students in the class who feel hesitant for various reasons. Methods like encouraging responses through zoom reactions and chat features, conducting activities and discussions in break out rooms of small groups and acknowledging small efforts can enhance the same.
3. Reimagining the Q/A While we have seen students being encouraged to ask any question they have in their mind, students still hesitate in asking those questions. To facilitate the same, The ‘Stupid’ Question Session and The Anonymous Q&A can be applied.
4. Musical Beginnings To facilitate an energetic start to every class in the virtual environment, music of the Professor’s choice can be played or something which goes along with the course subject. For example, a history class can start with a retro song, or a design thinking class with a high energy song!
5. Ungraded quizzes Platforms like Kahoot which focus on making learning fun can be used to take ungraded quizzes to increase engagement with the course matter. Raise Hand function of Zoom can also be used to organise a quiz through team divisions during the class.
6. Pulse Check-In A poll can be taken in every class to gauge the mood and presence of mind of students through a “How are you doing?” poll.
7. Personal anecdotes Students love to hear inspiring life stories of their Professors and get a flavour of the journey which can be a great engagement tactic!
8. GTK Questionnaire This is a Get to Know questionnaire that can be circulated by the Professor in the beginning of the course through Google Forms to get to know better
